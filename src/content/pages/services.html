Our Core Services

Comprehensive health services and personalised care

We cater to the health needs of your entire family. Here are some of the primary services Camberwellness Medical Practice provides.

  • Antenatal care
  • Child health and immunisations
  • Chronic disease management
  • Health assessments and preventive care
  • Iron infusions
  • Mental health
  • Minor procedures
  • Pain management
  • Sexual health
  • Skin cancer checks
  • Travel medicine and vaccinations
  • Employment medicals

Specialty Services

Camberwellness Medical Practice also offers a range of specialized services including pathology. Some of these may require a longer first appointment with your HCP to gauge your needs. Please call us if you have questions or require after-hours care.

  • Dietetics

    Our Accredited Practising Dietitican (APD) Tina Tran provides nutritional care and is committing to every client's healthcare goals. Her qualifications include Certified Prenatal Dietician (ELNA), Master of Dietetics (Deakin Uni) and Bachelor of Nutrition Science (Deakin).

    Her areas of expertise are healthy eating, weight management, nutrition, lifestyle, women's reproductive health, pediatrics, heart/liver/kidney health, digestive disorders, GI conditions, disability etc. She can also provide home visits.

  • Podiatry

    Our podiatrist Julian Jameson is a specialist in sports podiatry, diabetes management and rehabilition. With a Bachelor of Health Science and Masters of Podiatric Practice (LaTrobe Uni), he is experienced in community health, hospital and private settings.

    Julian is committed to getting the best outcomes for his clients and is a former athlete with a passion for sports-specific fitness and injury management.

  • Travel Medicine

    Dr. Robert Kumar is one of our GPs and a member of the Travel Medicine Alliance. Camberwellness Medical Practice is also an accredited yellow fever vaccine clinic. Your doctor can provide vaccines and health advice to prepare you for safe travel. When you book a 'travel appointment', you can discuss the details of your trip with your GP who will advise you on best practices.

    It is advisable to see your travel doctor 6 to 8 weeks before travelling, however a short notice visit may also suffice depending on the type of vaccine or medication you need.
